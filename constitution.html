Constitution of the Kingdom of Valoria

Preamble

We, the people of Valoria, united under our King and loyal to the Union of United Realms (UUR), establish this Constitution to preserve our sovereignty, ensure justice and security, and uphold the Valorian spirit of strength, honor, and freedom.

Article I — Valoria and the UUR

  1. Valoria is a proud and sovereign realm within the Union of United Realms (UUR).
  2. Valoria shall remain loyal to the UUR and contribute to its collective security and prosperity.
  3. In return, Valoria enjoys protection and recognition as a semi-autonomous member state.

Article II — Autonomy and Governance

  1. Valoria holds partial independence, retaining the right to create and enforce its own domestic laws consistent with UUR principles.
  2. The national government of Valoria shall operate as a constitutional monarchy with democratic elements.
  3. The King serves as Head of State and guardian of Valorian unity.
  4. The Lord of Alter Hafen acts as Deputy to the King and holds regional executive authority.
  5. A Valorian Representative Council (VRC) shall be elected by the citizens to deliberate and approve legislation.

Article III — Rights and Duties of Citizens

  1. All citizens are equal before the law and enjoy fundamental rights to safety, freedom of expression, property, and fair trial.
  2. Employment and contribution to the realm are civic duties and marks of honor.
  3. Citizens have the right to bear arms for defense of the realm, subject to regulation.
  4. The government may take exceptional measures in times of war or national emergency.

Article IV — Economy

  1. The Valorian economy shall promote self-reliance, innovation, and fair trade.
  2. Preference shall be given to Valorian and allied businesses when possible.
  3. Taxation shall be limited, ensuring citizens retain the fruits of their labor.
  4. Economic policy shall emphasize social responsibility and collective prosperity.

Article V — Security and Law Enforcement

  1. The Valorian Internal Service (VIS) shall maintain peace, order, and national integrity.
  2. The VIS functions as a branch of the National Defense Initiative (NDI) with its own Valorian command structure.
  3. Revival of traditional Valorian security principles (“King Adam’s Laws”) may be enacted to preserve discipline and justice.

Article VI — Defense and the Citizen Army

  1. The defense of Valoria is the duty of all able citizens.
  2. The government shall provide training, arms, and equipment to ensure readiness.
  3. The Valorian Army shall remain under joint command with the UUR in times of external war.

Article VII — Culture and Identity

  1. Valorian culture, language, and heritage shall be actively preserved and promoted.
  2. Other cultures shall be respected, provided they do not undermine Valorian identity.
  3. Acts of cultural mockery or deliberate provocation shall be prohibited by law.

Article VIII — Prohibited Ideologies

  1. Extremist ideologies threatening the unity or peace of Valoria — including communism or violent nationalist revivals — are banned.
  2. Propaganda promoting such ideas shall be treated as a criminal offense under Valorian law.

Article IX — Treason and Loyalty

  1. All officials and citizens shall swear loyalty to the Central Royal Family of the UUR and to the Valorian Crown.
  2. Treason, rebellion, or deliberate acts of subversion against Valoria or the UUR shall be punished in accordance with the law.

Article X — The Rule of Law

  1. All citizens and officials are bound by this Constitution.
  2. Laws may be enacted only by the VRC with approval of the King.
  3. The judicial system shall operate independently, ensuring fair trials and proportionate justice.

Article XI — Amendments

  1. This Constitution may be amended through a two-thirds majority vote in the VRC and approval by the King.
  2. Amendments must remain consistent with the fundamental principles of Valoria and the UUR.

⚖️ The Valorian Law Codex (VLC)

Established under the Constitution of the Kingdom of Valoria, ratified by the Valorian Royal Council (VRC) and confirmed by His Majesty the King.

Title I — General Provisions

Article 1 — Purpose

The Valorian Law Codex ensures order, justice, and the protection of Valoria’s sovereignty, culture, and citizens.

Article 2 — Supremacy of the Constitution

All laws derive their authority from the Valorian Constitution. In conflict, constitutional principles prevail.

Article 3 — Equality Before the Law

All Valorians are equal before the law regardless of heritage, belief, or station. Nobility and office may grant duties, not immunity.

Article 4 — Application of Justice

Justice shall be fair, swift, and proportional. Punishment serves rehabilitation and deterrence — not humiliation.

Title II — Crimes Against the Realm

Chapter 1 — Treason and Rebellion

Article 5.1 — High Treason
Acts intending to overthrow, harm, or betray Valoria or the UUR are treason.
→ Punishment: 10–30 days imprisonment, exile, or, in grave cases, loss of citizenship.

Article 5.2 — Sedition and Civil Disorder
Organizing or leading demonstrations that oppose royal decrees or promote disobedience constitutes sedition.
→ Punishment: 3–15 days imprisonment or fine up to $5,000.

Article 5.3 — Foreign Collaboration
Cooperating with foreign or hostile powers against Valoria’s interest is a grave offense.
→ Punishment: 15–30 days imprisonment and asset seizure.

Chapter 2 — Symbolic Acts and Loyalty

Article 6.1 — Insulting the Realm
Publicly mocking Valoria, its flag, anthem, or institutions is prohibited.
→ Punishment: $500–$2,000 fine or 3–10 days service under civic authority.

Article 6.2 — Royal Disrespect
Insulting or slandering the King, the Valorian Royal Family, or allied monarchs is illegal.
→ Punishment: $2,000 fine or community service benefiting royal institutions.

Article 6.3 — Anti-Traditionalist Activity (“Con-Valo”)
Raising or displaying the pre-reform Valorian flag or mocking Valorian identity to provoke unrest constitutes subversion.
→ Punishment: $500–$3,000 fine or up to 7 days imprisonment.

Title III — Civil and Social Order

Chapter 1 — Conduct and Respect

Article 7.1 — Disrespect Toward Authority
Refusing lawful orders or verbally insulting officials is an offense.
→ Punishment: $300 fine or 2–5 days service.

Article 7.2 — Threats and Harassment
Threatening, intimidating, or coercing others is forbidden.
→ Punishment: $500 fine or up to 7 days imprisonment.

Article 7.3 — Destruction of Property
Intentional damage to property (public or private) is a criminal act.
→ Punishment: Reimbursement of damages + $2,000 fine or community restoration work.

Chapter 2 — Theft, Fraud, and Deception

Article 8.1 — Theft
Taking property without consent is illegal.
→ Punishment: Return of goods and repayment of 125% value, or imprisonment up to 10 days.

Article 8.2 — Fraud
Deceiving others for personal gain is prohibited.
→ Punishment: Restitution + $1,000–$3,000 fine or up to 12 days imprisonment.

Article 8.3 — Exploitation of Loopholes
Abusing unclear or missing laws to gain unfair advantage undermines justice.
→ Punishment: Penalty set by the judiciary, proportional to damage done.

Chapter 3 — Public Morality and Health

Article 9.1 — Drug Prohibition
Production, trade, or use of illegal narcotics is banned.
→ Punishment: 5–18 days imprisonment; confiscation of profits and substances.
Exemption: Licensed individuals (medical or scientific).

Article 9.2 — Disorderly Conduct
Public intoxication, violence, or indecent acts disrupt community peace.
→ Punishment: $300–$1,000 fine or 1–5 days civic service.

Title IV — Economic and Administrative Laws

Article 10 — Economic Loyalty
Valorian and allied enterprises shall be given preference in contracts and trade.
Attempts to sabotage domestic production or employment are punishable.

Article 11 — Corruption and Abuse of Office
Officials misusing power or taking bribes face severe penalty.
→ Punishment: Dismissal, $5,000–$10,000 fine, and up to 15 days imprisonment.

Article 12 — Tax and Contribution
Though Valoria imposes minimal taxation, mandatory civic contributions or service hours may be enacted in times of need.

Title V — Rights and Safeguards

Article 13 — Right to Fair Trial
Every accused has the right to defense, legal representation, and appeal.

Article 14 — Privacy and Dignity
Citizens’ private correspondence, homes, and property are protected from unjust intrusion.

Article 15 — Emergency Suspension of Rights
In times of war or national emergency, certain rights may be temporarily limited by royal decree, subject to later VRC review.

Title VI — Implementation and Interpretation

Article 16 — Judiciary Authority
The Royal Court of Valoria interprets this Codex. Its rulings set binding precedent.

Article 17 — Enforcement
The Valorian Internal Service (VIS) enforces this Codex under oversight from the Ministry of Justice and Security.

Article 18 — Amendments to the Codex
Laws may be modified by the VRC with approval of the King.

Title VII — Symbolic and Cultural Protection

Article 19 — National Heritage
Vandalizing monuments, memorials, or cultural landmarks is forbidden.
→ Punishment: $1,000–$5,000 fine or 10 days restoration duty.

Article 20 — Cultural Integrity
Acts of deliberate cultural provocation or spreading hate against Valorian identity are offenses.
→ Punishment: Up to $3,000 fine or civic re-education program.

Closing Clause

Let this Codex serve not as an instrument of fear but as a guardian of order, pride, and unity. Justice shall be firm yet righteous — for the strength of Valoria lies not only in its arms, but in its fairness.
